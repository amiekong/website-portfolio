My journey into data science

I remember attending my first hackathon at an arena with over 1,000 students that would spend their entire weekend collaborating and coding. It was all foreign to me so I navigated towards what I felt most comfortable with and networked with sponsor companies about a field I had no knowledge in. At the end of the day, I didn’t win any grand prizes but I did gain something even more valuable: finding a career that was inspiring. Either the companies did a great job recruiting or maybe I was fascinated by all the cool tech gadgets.

I taught myself Python and felt a surge of excitement having finished my first for-loop that didn’t get stuck. As a pre-dental student at the time, I combined my knowledge of organic chemistry and this newfound skill to create a simple tool that would classify the product of different combinations of chemical reagents. That ability to create a useful tool from one programming course and tying it with knowledge from another field is what won me over. And that is when I knew I had to transition to tech.
